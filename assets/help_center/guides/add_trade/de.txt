Guide: Logging Your Trades (Part 1)
This section powers your journal. Accurate input here yields professional‑grade performance insights.

1. Trade direction
- Buy · Long (green): Used when you bought the asset.
- Sell · Short (black): Used when you sold the asset short.

2. Asset selection & customisation
- Market selector: Category (Forex, Index, Future, Crypto, Stock, Commo).
- Asset field: Symbol (e.g. EUR/USD).
- ⚙️ If the symbol is missing, use customisation to add it manually.

3. Volume & quick entry
- Size (lots): Enter position size.
- Lot shortcuts: Buttons (0.01–1) for speed and fewer typos.

4. Price, time & context
- Entry & Exit: Entry and exit prices.
- Time picker: Exact times. Paychek uses them for hold time and session stats.

Precision checkboxes:
- Breakeven: Mark if the trade closed flat.
- Before / After News: Tag trades around economic releases.

5. P&L (capital & gain)
Live account impact:
- Profit/Loss: Net P&L from your fills.
- ⚙️ Fees: Gear next to P&L to set commissions.
- Percentage: Paychek shows return vs capital (e.g. +2.44%) for risk control.

[img:assets/help_center/performance_volume_lot_size.png]

⏱️ Why exact entry & exit time matters
For advanced analytics, log precise times whenever possible.

Why it matters:
- Hold time: Correlates duration with win rate.
- Market sessions: Europe, US, evening buckets.
- Plan violations: Counts trades outside your defined windows.

Paychek tip: Treat time as data, not busywork — it reveals your real edge.

Guide: Discipline & strategy (Part 2)
The right-hand panel analyses behaviour — luck vs discipline.

[img:assets/help_center/add_trade_discipline_principe.png]

1. Principle vs Feeling (critical choice)
Before saving, be honest about the trigger:

Principle mode (green): You followed plan and predefined rules.

Feeling mode (red): Emotion-led (FOMO, revenge tilt), unvalidated intuition, or reactive volatility chasing.

Note: Feeling mode locks strategy fields by default. Unlock via ⚙️ “Discipline settings” if you still want structured notes.

2. Discipline slider (%)
Plans slip. Move the slider 0–100% to grade how cleanly you executed.

Use it to see “forced” entries vs disciplined execution.

[img:assets/help_center/add_trade_discipline_strategy_execution.png]

3. Strategy (setup) selection
Pick the playbook that matched (e.g. SMC order block vs breakout).

List comes from your My Strategy setups.

4. Points to review
Checklist discipline: Tick lines you violated (e.g. “R:R 1:2 not met”, “Overtrading”).

Checked faults show in red under “Saved choices”.

Impact: Rolls straight into Performance so you see which lapse costs money.

[img:assets/help_center/add_trade_discipline_feeling_header.png]

5. Discipline settings (⚙️)
Gear opens Discipline settings:

Feeling mode: Allow or forbid strategy panels while in Feeling mode.

[img:assets/help_center/add_trade_discipline_settings_feeling_mode.png]

“Principle vs Feeling” shows cold numbers—not judgment—that discipline compounds.

Guide: Custom discipline (Part 3)
Paychek scores you against YOUR rules—not generic fluff.

[img:assets/help_center/add_trade_discipline_part3_mobile.png]

1. My Analysis plan
Tracks technical cleanliness (SMC, chart patterns, indicators, etc.).
- Respect % slider: Score setup quality versus your norms.
- Dynamic pickers: Mirrors items defined in My Analysis (resistance, timeframe, phase…).
- Error trace: Tick missed items — Paychek tallies recurring technical leaks.

2. Checklist (session plan)
Conduct & risk housekeeping.
- Plan respect slider for the session overall.
- Custom items created in Checklist (stop placed, no overtrading, R:R respected…).
- Missed items instantly show red crosses under recorded choices — your discipline mirror.

3. Current State (mental)
- State of the moment (confidence, stress, fatigue…). Customise categories in Mental State.
- Tie-in: Discover patterns like “80% win-rate when calm, 10% when hyped.”

4. Quick tags (FOMO · Tilt/Revenge · Blind)
- FOMO: Fear of missing a move.
- Tilt / revenge: Trading to recover losses.
- Blind: No real preparation.

Impact: Performance quantifies the dollar cost of each emotional trigger.

Guide: Proof, CSV & save (Part 4)
Finalize with visuals and imports.

[img:assets/help_center/add_trade_part4_screenshot_csv_save.png]

1. Screenshot proof
Strong journals stay visual.

Tap “Tap to add an image” with your chart screenshot.

Stored images propagate to Trade recap, trade PDF archives, and performance reports.

[img:assets/help_center/add_trade_part4_trade_recap.png]

2. CSV import (automation)
Skip manual taps when possible.
- Pick your platform and tap IMPORT CSV — prices, lots, times fill automatically.
- Focus on mindset & discipline—not data entry grunt work.

3. Validate & save
- Save: Store trade + PDF artefacts, returns to journal.
- Save & next: Saves and opens fresh form—ideal when logging sequences fast.
